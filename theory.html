Thumb Layout Theory

This is going to be a very rough briefing on all the theory that I am aware of on thumb layouts. This will cover single-thumb only and will not include magic (and other multi-input or multi-layer layouts).

Letters for Thumb

The most commonly used letters for thumb layouts are E, R, S, T, and N. For this reason, I will discuss these letters first, then I will alphabetically discuss the rest.

E: The most commonly used thumb letter all the way back to Maltron, it is a fundamentally logical choice. It is the most frequent of any letter, and interacts with everything. Removing this letter should lead to the lowest SFBs possible - and it does!... theoretically. In practice, it creates a layout with such high SFSs that the about 0.03% decrease over the best of R (which has reasonable SFSs) just isn't worth it. Furthermore, when implemented incorrectly, it tends to induce a LOT of redirects. Finally, in addition to all of these, EE is very common - being the third most common. For most people, while the thumb is very strong, it is not particularly fast; thus making it unideal to quickly type out a double letter.
