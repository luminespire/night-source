Thumb Layout Theory

This is going to be a very rough briefing on all the theory that I am aware of on thumb layouts. This will cover single-thumb only and will not include magic (and other multi-input or multi-layer layouts).

Letters for Thumb

The most commonly used letters for thumb layouts are E, R, S, and N. For this reason, I will discuss these letters first, then I will alphabetically discuss the rest.

E: The most commonly used thumb letter - used all the way back on the Maltron - E is a fundamentally logical choice. It is the most frequent of any letter, and interacts with everything (thus causing SFBs and SFSs). Removing this letter should lead to the lowest SFBs possible - and it does!... Theoretically. In practice, it creates a layout with such high SFSs that the about 0.03% decrease over the best of R (which has reasonable SFSs) just is not worth it. Furthermore, when implemented incorrectly, it tends to induce a LOT of redirects (again due to its frequency). Finally, in addition to all of these, EE is the third most common repeat bigram. For most people, while the thumb is very strong, it is not particularly fast; thus making it unideal to quickly type out a double letter. All in all, while E may seem tempting and logical to use (and in certain cases it still is), for the majority of people, other options are simply better.

R: Generally accepted as the best letter (although better put, best researched), R is similar to E in the way that it interacts with everything, but comes at the advantage of not being hyper common (thus not causing so many redirects) and not repeating commonly. If you want 0% SFBs on Monkeytype (English-200), this is the most reasonable way to do it [Night does this!].

Using R on thumb pushes L and H to be paired together (and thus usually M) if you want competitive statistics. Thus, LHM | DTK structure (or in the case of Night LH | DTMK) is forced making designs rather limited. This is primarily because L is unable to pair with anything else effectively.

S: Currently being experimented with, S is unique in that it purposely reintroduces a semi-common SFB, RL | LR

, in order to achieve a better structure (by freeing up H). It will inherently have worse SFBs as removing RL | LR was the last major removable SFB (other than the vowels). However, it does tend to have lower SFSs.
