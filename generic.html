Terminology

All examples are in the QWERTY layout.

SFB

Plural: SFBs. Stands for Same F>inger Bigram. Describes when the same finger is used to press two keys. Example: ED.

SFS/dSFB

Plural: SFSs/dSFBs. Stands for Same F>inger Sipgram/disjointed Same F>inger Bigram. Describes when the same finger is used to press two keys, however, there is key in between the two that does not use the same finger. Example: GOT

Alternation

Short: Alt. Describes when a series of keys are pressed by opposite hands one after another. Example: FIGHT.

Redirect*

Short: Red | Plural: Redirects/Reds. Describes key sequences against a common direction on one hand. Example: YOU.

Bad Redirect

Short: Bad Red | Plural: Bad Redirects/Bad Reds. Describes key sequences against a common direction not involving an index finger on one hand. Example: SAD.

Roll

Plural: Rolls. Describes two key presses on the same hand with different fingers, followed by another keypress on the other hand. Example: POT.

Inroll/Outroll

Plural: Inrolls/Outrolls. Describes a type of roll, inrolls involve the two key presses moving towards the center of the keyboard, while outrolls involve the two key presses moving away. Example: POT (in) vs OPT (out).

Onehand

Short: One. Describes key sequences moving in the same direction on one hand. Can also be described as in or out. Example: JKL (in) vs LKJ (out).

LSB

Plural: LSBs. Stands for Lateral Stretch Bigram. Describes two keys being pressed by adjacent fingers, but requires a horizontal stretch of one key length (or more) between the fingers. Example: ET.

Scissor**

Short: FSB (Full Scissor Bigram) | Plural: Scissors. Describes two keys being pressed by adjacent fingers, but requires the fingers to be on opposite rows. Example: WZ.

Half Scissors***

Short: HSB (Half Scissor Bigram) | Plural: Half-Scissors. Describes two keys being pressed by adjacent fingers, but one finger is on the homerow while another is not. Example: DR.

nU ___

Describes a special varient of some terminology that extends beyond one key length. Example: 2U SFS = SFS that requires the repeated finger to move two key units, 1.6U LSB = LSB that requires a 1.6U seperation between the fingers, etc.

Additional Notes

* Redirects generally affects low speeds and become less impactful at high speeds.

** Effects are still debated upon. No impact on speed for sure, but comfort is arguable.

*** Most do not consider as an issue.
