Night

A thumb alpha utilizing keyboard layout.

Design

Night is designed to have good statistics while maintaining a cohesive layout.

SFB & SFS

Night features extremely low SFBs and good SFS. These enable Night to be an efficient layout at high speeds.

Alternation

Night is an alternation heavy layout. This makes typing feel consistent and rhythmic; albeit not for everyone.

Redirects

Night focuses heavily on minimizing redirects, particularly bad redirects. This reduces awkward sequences, again helping typing feel consistent.

Effort Map

Night specifically places high usage letters on strong and dexterit fingers. This helps balance typing load and take advantage of the characteristics of each finger.

C Index

Night uses a C index to minimize SFBs. This also takes advantage of the dexterity of index finger and the addition keys it can access.

Vowel Stacks

Night uses a traditional AO EU punctuation I vowel block to maximize statistics. AO EU is chosen for effort map, although other varients will work just fine.
